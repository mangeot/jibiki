Système d'harmonisation du langage juridique pour l'environnement, et l'aménagement du territoire au sein des alpes multilingues

The LexALP project intends to contribute to supranational communication by responding to the requirement of greater univocity and clarity expressed by organizations in the field in particular by the Alpine Convention, in order to promote the economic, social and cultural development of the Alpine arc. The project's objective is therefore to harmonise the terminology of the Convention's four languages (French, German, Italian and Slovenian) so that the member states are able to cooperate effectively, surpassing the obstacles posed by the disputes over the conception and execution of common politics in the fields of environment and spatial planning. 

The methodologies and technological instruments developed by LexALP will help compare the specialised terminology of six different national legal systems in four different languages, and to harmonise it, optimising the understanding between the various Alpine states in environmental matters at the supranational level. 

In order to reach its objective, LexALP can take confidence in the various specialty areas (linguistics, law and computer science) of its partners, who are active as university institutes, research centers and public agencies. The project's activities will be supported by prestigious institutions like the Alpine Convention, the Délégation Générale de Langue Francaise e des Langues de France (Ministère de la Culture et de la Communication), the  Bundesministerium für Land- und Forstwirtschaft, Umwelt und Wasserwirtschaft , the Autonomous Region of Aosta, the Office for Linguistic Issues of the Autonomous Province of Bolzano). 

LexALP is a project planned for a duration of three years (2005-2007),  and is financed in the context of the INTERREG IIIB Alpine Space programme.
